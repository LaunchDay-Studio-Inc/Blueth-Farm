## Overview

### Game Identity
**Title:** Blueth Farm  
**Working Title:** Tidal Harvest  
**Elevator Pitch:** "Stardew Valley meets climate science — restore degraded coastal ecosystems, build a thriving blue carbon farm, sequester carbon, and save your community from rising seas."

### Core Details
- **Genre:** Cozy simulation / management game with narrative depth
- **Target Platforms:** PC, Console, Mobile (stretch)
- **Art Style:** Stylized 2D isometric with rich tidal animations (think Spiritfarer meets Stardew Valley)
- **Target Audience:** 
  - Fans of Stardew Valley, Spiritfarer, Terra Nil, Alba: A Wildlife Adventure
  - Environmentally-conscious gamers
  - Ages 10+
  - Players seeking meaningful, educational gaming experiences
- **Estimated Playtime:** 40-60 hours main story, unlimited sandbox/legacy mode

### Core Pillars
1. **Restoration is Rewarding:** Transform degraded coastlines into thriving ecosystems
2. **Science-Grounded Fantasy:** Real blue carbon science made engaging and accessible
3. **Community Impact:** Your actions directly benefit the struggling coastal town
4. **Cozy Complexity:** Approachable gameplay with deep, interconnected systems
5. **Legacy & Hope:** Build something lasting that matters beyond the game

## Setting & Narrative

### The Hook
You inherit a degraded coastal property from your marine-biologist grandmother, who recently passed away. The property sits at the edge of a struggling coastal town where fisheries are collapsing, storms are intensifying, and the shoreline is eroding away.

### The Vision
Your grandmother's journal reveals her lifelong dream: to prove that restoring blue carbon ecosystems isn't just good for the planet — it can be a viable livelihood. She believed that regenerating the coast could sequester carbon, protect the community, and restore the local economy.

### Narrative Structure
- **Discovery-Based Storytelling:** The narrative unfolds through journal entries discovered throughout the game
- **Environmental Context:** Each season and storm reminds players why this work matters
- **Community Transformation:** NPCs' stories evolve as your restoration efforts show results
- **Personal Journey:** Themes of legacy, stewardship, hope, and honoring the past while building the future

### Key Themes
- **Legacy:** Continuing your grandmother's vision and leaving your own mark
- **Stewardship:** Being a caretaker of the land and sea
- **Hope:** Environmental action can make a tangible difference
- **Community Resilience:** Working together to adapt to climate challenges
- **Indigenous Knowledge:** Incorporating traditional ecological wisdom
- **Science & Heart:** Balancing data with emotional connection to place

## World Zones

The game world is divided into five interconnected coastal zones, each representing a distinct blue carbon ecosystem. Players unlock zones progressively as they master restoration techniques and expand their operations.

### Zone 1: The Shallows
**Ecosystem Type:** Seagrass meadows  
**Unlock Condition:** Starting zone (Year 1)  
**Water Depth:** 0-3 meters at high tide  

**Characteristics:**
- Clear, shallow waters with sandy substrate
- Gentle wave action
- High light penetration
- Moderate tidal influence

**Key Species:**
- **Plants:** Zostera marina (eelgrass), Thalassia testudinum (turtlegrass), Halodule wrightii (shoal grass)
- **Wildlife:** Blue crabs, seahorses, juvenile fish, sand dollars, moon jellyfish
- **Birds:** Pelicans, terns, sandpipers

**Mechanics:**
- Learn basic planting and growth monitoring
- Understand tidal cycles
- First carbon sequestration measurements
- Introduction to sediment quality

**Challenges:**
- Shallow water limits planting times to high tide
- Susceptible to storms and sediment disturbance
- Competition from invasive algae

### Zone 2: The Mudflats
**Ecosystem Type:** Salt marshes  
**Unlock Condition:** Mid game (Year 2) — Complete 50% Shallows restoration  
**Elevation:** Intertidal zone (exposed at low tide)

**Characteristics:**
- Rich, muddy substrate
- Dramatic tidal exposure
- High nutrient retention
- Dense root systems

**Key Species:**
- **Plants:** Spartina alterniflora (smooth cordgrass), Salicornia (glasswort), Juncus roemerianus (black needlerush)
- **Wildlife:** Fiddler crabs, ribbed mussels, diamondback terrapins, marsh wrens
- **Birds:** Great blue herons, egrets, rails

**Mechanics:**
- Hydrological management (controlling water flow)
- Sediment accretion monitoring
- Carbon storage in deep peat soils
- Creating living shorelines

**Challenges:**
- Must plant during specific tidal windows
- Managing freshwater/saltwater balance
- Preventing erosion during establishment

### Zone 3: The Estuary
**Ecosystem Type:** Mangrove forests  
**Unlock Condition:** Mid-late game (Year 3) — Establish thriving salt marsh + town investment  
**Water Type:** Brackish (mixing fresh and salt water)

**Characteristics:**
- Complex root systems above waterline
- Sheltered, calm waters
- High biodiversity
- Filtered light through canopy

**Key Species:**
- **Plants:** Rhizophora mangle (red mangrove), Avicennia germinans (black mangrove), Laguncularia racemosa (white mangrove)
- **Wildlife:** Mangrove snapper, tarpon, oysters, manatees, crocodiles (in some regions)
- **Birds:** Roseate spoonbills, wood storks, ospreys

**Mechanics:**
- Propagule collection and nursery management
- Multi-layered canopy restoration
- Coastal storm protection simulation
- Habitat connectivity between zones

**Challenges:**
- Long growth cycles (years to maturity)
- Precise salinity requirements
- Vulnerability to frost events (in temperate regions)

### Zone 4: The Reef Edge
**Ecosystem Type:** Kelp forests  
**Unlock Condition:** Late game (Year 4) — Achieve high biodiversity score + unlock research  
**Water Depth:** 5-25 meters

**Characteristics:**
- Rocky substrate
- Cool, nutrient-rich waters
- Strong currents
- Vertical forest structure

**Key Species:**
- **Plants:** Macrocystis pyrifera (giant kelp), Nereocystis luetkeana (bull kelp), Laminaria (oarweed)
- **Wildlife:** Sea urchins, sea otters, rockfish, harbor seals, octopus
- **Birds:** Sea otters, cormorants (surface feeders)

**Mechanics:**
- Managing urchin populations to prevent overgrazing
- Kelp harvesting for sustainable products
- Water temperature monitoring
- Carbon export to deep ocean

**Challenges:**
- Kelp vulnerable to warm water events
- Requires boat access for planting and maintenance
- Balancing harvest with ecosystem health

### Zone 5: The Deep
**Ecosystem Type:** Open ocean / carbon sinks  
**Unlock Condition:** Endgame (Year 5+) — Master all ecosystems + complete research tree  
**Water Depth:** 25+ meters

**Characteristics:**
- Deep blue waters
- Minimal light penetration
- Long-term carbon storage
- Connection to global ocean currents

**Key Species:**
- **Wildlife:** Whales, dolphins, manta rays, deep-sea fish, bioluminescent organisms
- **Birds:** Albatross, shearwaters (flying over)

**Mechanics:**
- Deploying deep-water monitoring stations
- Tracking carbon export from coastal ecosystems
- Understanding the "blue carbon conveyor belt"
- Whale conservation and carbon sequestration link

**Challenges:**
- Limited direct intervention (more observation-based)
- Expensive equipment requirements
- Weather-dependent access

### Zone Interconnections
All zones are ecologically connected:
- **Nutrient Flow:** Upstream zones provide nutrients to downstream zones
- **Species Migration:** Fish breed in mangroves, mature in kelp forests
- **Carbon Transfer:** Coastal ecosystems export carbon to deep ocean
- **Storm Protection:** Each zone protects inland zones from wave energy
- **Symbiosis Bonuses:** Healthy adjacent zones boost each other's productivity

## Core Gameplay Systems

### 1. Planting & Restoration System

**Core Loop:** Scout → Prepare → Plant → Monitor → Maintain

**Planting Mechanics:**
- **Sediment Preparation:** Till and condition substrate before planting
- **Species Selection:** Choose appropriate species for depth, salinity, and season
- **Planting Tools:** Upgrade from hand planting to mechanical planters
- **Spacing & Density:** Optimal spacing increases survival and growth rates
- **Transplanting:** Move established plants to fill gaps or create new beds

**Nursery System:**
- **Seed Collection:** Harvest seeds and propagules from mature plants
- **Germination:** Controlled environment for starting young plants
- **Grow-Out Tanks:** Raise seedlings to transplant size
- **Hardening Off:** Acclimate nursery plants to field conditions
- **Rare Species:** Unlock heritage varieties through exploration and research

**Growth Simulation:**
- **Real-Time Growth:** Plants grow in real-time based on conditions
- **Environmental Factors:** Light, temperature, salinity, nutrients, sediment quality
- **Seasonal Variation:** Growth rates change with seasons
- **Maturity Stages:** Seedling → Juvenile → Mature → Reproductive
- **Senescence:** Old plants die naturally, releasing nutrients

**Soil/Sediment Quality:**
- **Organic Content:** Higher organic matter = better carbon storage
- **Compaction:** Affects root penetration and water movement
- **Nutrients:** N, P, K levels affect growth rates
- **Contamination:** Pollution reduces ecosystem health
- **Improvement:** Add compost, manage water flow, use bioremediators

**Propagation Methods:**
- **Seeding:** Broadcast seeds for meadows and marshes
- **Plugs:** Individual transplants for precision planting
- **Rhizome Fragments:** Vegetative propagation for seagrasses
- **Propagules:** Mangrove seedlings that grow on parent tree
- **Spore Release:** Kelp reproduction mechanics

### 2. Tidal & Seasonal System

**Tidal Mechanics:**
- **Two Tides Daily:** High and low tide cycle every 12.4 hours (real-world timing accelerated)
- **Tidal Range:** Varies based on lunar phase (spring tides vs. neap tides)
- **Accessibility:** Some areas only accessible at low tide, others only at high tide
- **Planting Windows:** Optimal planting during specific tidal stages
- **Harvest Timing:** Low tide exposes intertidal species for harvest

**Lunar Cycle:**
- **28-Day Cycle:** New moon → Full moon → New moon
- **Spring Tides:** New/full moon = highest high and lowest low tides
- **Neap Tides:** Quarter moons = smallest tidal range
- **Spawning Events:** Many species spawn on full moons
- **Enhanced Growth:** Certain plants grow faster during specific moon phases

**Seasonal System:**
- **Four Seasons:** Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter (or region-appropriate variations)
- **Day Length:** Longer days in summer, shorter in winter
- **Water Temperature:** Affects species activity and growth
- **Storm Frequency:** More storms in fall/winter
- **Species Migration:** Seasonal arrivals and departures
- **Optimal Planting Seasons:** Different species prefer different planting times

**Weather System:**
- **Daily Weather:** Sunny, cloudy, rainy, stormy, foggy
- **Climate Events:**
  - **Storm Surges:** Test your coastal protection systems
  - **Heatwaves:** Stress ecosystems, potential die-offs
  - **Algal Blooms:** Triggered by warm water + nutrients
  - **Cold Snaps:** Can damage tropical species
  - **Droughts:** Affect salinity and freshwater inputs

**Forecasting Tools:**
- **Basic:** Simple 3-day forecast (starting tool)
- **Advanced:** 7-day forecast with probability (research unlock)
- **Seasonal Predictions:** Long-range climate forecasts (late-game)
- **Tide Charts:** Always available reference
- **Storm Warning System:** Alerts for major events

**Day/Night Cycle:**
- **24-Hour Cycle:** Accelerated time (configurable)
- **Lighting Changes:** Dynamic lighting from dawn to dusk
- **Species Behavior:** Nocturnal vs. diurnal species
- **Player Activities:** Some activities better suited for day or night
- **Energy Management:** Player stamina regenerates through rest

### 3. Carbon Ledger System (Unique Core Hook)

**Living Carbon Dashboard:**

The carbon tracking system is the game's signature mechanic — a beautiful, educational visualization of your environmental impact.

**Core Metrics:**
- **Total CO₂ Sequestered:** Cumulative carbon captured since farm start
- **Annual Sequestration Rate:** Tons CO₂ captured per year
- **Carbon in Biomass:** Short-term storage in living plants
- **Carbon in Sediment:** Long-term storage (millennial timescales)
- **Avoided Emissions:** Carbon that would have been released without your restoration

**Dashboard Visualizations:**
- **Living Graph:** Animated line graph showing carbon accumulation over time
- **Ecosystem Breakdown:** Pie chart showing contribution by zone
- **Projection Curves:** Forecast future sequestration based on current ecosystems
- **Comparison Metrics:** Your farm vs. global averages, previous years
- **Milestone Celebrations:** Visual fireworks when hitting major carbon goals

**Carbon Credit System:**
- **Verification Process:** Must reach ecosystem health threshold for certification
- **Credit Generation:** 1 credit = 1 ton CO₂ sequestered
- **Market Sales:** Sell credits on fluctuating in-game market
- **Price Factors:** Supply/demand, global events, policy changes
- **Premium Credits:** Higher prices for co-benefits (biodiversity, community impact)

**Market Dynamics:**
- **Base Price:** $15-50 per credit (reflecting real-world variability)
- **Market Events:** 
  - Policy announcements increase demand
  - Natural disasters spike prices
  - Oversupply crashes market temporarily
  - Corporate net-zero pledges boost long-term prices
- **Trading Strategy:** Sell immediately vs. wait for better prices

**Real-World Equivalency Display:**
- "Your farm offset the equivalent of X cars for a year"
- "That's the same as planting X trees"
- "You've prevented X tons of ice from melting"
- "Your ecosystems cleaned enough CO₂ for X people's annual emissions"

**Optional Global Leaderboard:**
- **Total Carbon Sequestered:** Worldwide farm rankings
- **Seasonal Challenges:** Monthly competitions
- **Regional Rankings:** Compare with other coastal farms
- **Achievement Badges:** Special recognition for milestones
- **Privacy Options:** Opt-in/out of leaderboard

### 4. Ecosystem & Biodiversity Management

**Wildlife Attraction System:**

Healthy ecosystems naturally attract wildlife, creating a positive feedback loop.

**Species Tiers:**
- **Foundation Species:** Core plants (seagrass, mangroves, etc.)
- **Primary Consumers:** Herbivores and filter feeders (urchins, crabs, oysters)
- **Secondary Consumers:** Small predators (fish, birds)
- **Apex Predators:** Top of food chain (dolphins, sea otters, large fish)
- **Charismatic Megafauna:** Rare, special species (manatees, sea turtles, whales)

**Biodiversity Score:**
- **Calculation:** Species richness × evenness × ecosystem health
- **Range:** 0-100 points
- **Thresholds:**
  - 0-20: Degraded
  - 21-40: Recovering
  - 41-60: Healthy
  - 61-80: Thriving
  - 81-100: Pristine
- **Benefits:** Higher scores = greater resilience, carbon sequestration bonus, tourism income

**Food Web Mechanics:**
- **Trophic Cascades:** Removing predators causes prey population explosions
- **Overharvesting Consequences:** Take too many crabs → urchins overpopulate → kelp overgrazed
- **Balance Indicators:** Visual warnings when populations get out of balance
- **Restoration Strategies:** Reintroduce missing species to restore balance

**Invasive Species Management:**
- **Common Invaders:** Invasive algae, lionfish, European green crabs
- **Detection:** Monitoring reveals invasive arrivals
- **Removal Methods:** Manual removal, biological control, habitat modification
- **Persistence:** Ongoing management required, can't be eradicated permanently
- **Economic Cost:** Resources spent on invasive control vs. restoration

**Species Catalog/Codex:**
- **Discovery-Based:** Species added as you encounter them
- **Scientific Information:** Real facts about each species
  - Common and scientific names
  - Habitat preferences
  - Diet and behavior
  - Conservation status
  - Role in ecosystem
  - Cultural significance
- **Beautiful Illustrations:** High-quality art for each species
- **Progress Tracking:** X/Y species discovered per zone

**Symbiosis Bonuses:**
- **Classic Pairs:** 
  - Sea otters + kelp forests (otters control urchins)
  - Oysters + seagrass (oysters filter water, improving seagrass health)
  - Mangroves + fish nurseries (juvenile fish = healthier adult populations)
- **Bonus Effects:** 
  - Growth rate increases
  - Carbon sequestration bonuses
  - Resilience to disturbance
  - Aesthetic improvements

**Ecosystem Health Indicators:**
- **Water Clarity:** Visual transparency, affects light penetration
- **Species Count:** Total number of species present
- **Sediment Stability:** Resistance to erosion
- **Nutrient Cycling:** Efficiency of nutrient use
- **Oxygen Levels:** Dissolved oxygen in water
- **pH Balance:** Ocean acidification tracking
- **Temperature:** Optimal range for species

### 5. Community & Economy System

**Town Economy:**

The nearby coastal town serves as your economic and social hub.

**Market System:**
- **Sustainable Harvest Sales:**
  - Oysters, clams, mussels
  - Edible seaweed (kelp, dulse)
  - Sustainably caught fish
  - Salt marsh lamb (late-game)
  - Eco-tourism experiences
- **Dynamic Pricing:** Supply/demand curves + seasonal variation
- **Quality Grades:** Better ecosystem health = higher quality products = premium prices
- **Market Days:** Weekly market creates rhythm and planning opportunities

**Town Investment:**
- **Infrastructure Projects:**
  - Rebuild the dock (unlocks commercial fishing revival)
  - Fund marine research lab (unlocks advanced research)
  - Open eco-tourism center (generates passive income)
  - Restore community center (unlocks festivals)
  - Build climate education center (unlocks youth programs)
- **Investment Returns:** Each project provides gameplay benefits and story progression
- **Community Funding:** Some projects require matching funds from town (reputation gates)

**NPC System:**

Six core NPCs with deep story arcs:

**1. Old Salt — The Fisherman**
- **Role:** Traditional knowledge keeper, skeptic-turned-believer
- **Story Arc:** Teaches traditional fishing wisdom → sees his catches improve as ecosystems recover → becomes restoration advocate
- **Gameplay:** Teaches fishing spots, identifies species, shares weather lore
- **Friendship Rewards:** Fishing equipment, boat upgrades, historical site locations

**2. Dr. Marina Chen — The Climate Scientist**
- **Role:** Research partner, technical advisor
- **Story Arc:** University researcher studying blue carbon → partners with you for field site → publishes groundbreaking paper on your farm
- **Gameplay:** Unlocks research projects, explains science, provides monitoring equipment
- **Friendship Rewards:** Research point bonuses, advanced tools, scientific publications

**3. Mayor Stormwell — The Skeptical Politician**
- **Role:** Political antagonist-turned-ally
- **Story Arc:** Doubts restoration economics → sees tax revenue increase → becomes climate champion
- **Gameplay:** Town investment approval, policy changes, permits
- **Friendship Rewards:** Grants and subsidies, policy bonuses, land access

**4. Kai — The Young Activist**
- **Role:** Community organizer, youth voice
- **Story Arc:** Frustrated by inaction → energized by visible results → organizes regional movement
- **Gameplay:** Volunteers for labor, organizes beach cleanups, spreads word about your farm
- **Friendship Rewards:** Free labor days, community volunteers, social media boosts to tourism

**5. Elder Riverwatcher — The Indigenous Elder**
- **Role:** Traditional ecological knowledge, spiritual connection
- **Story Arc:** Shares ancestral land management practices → sees ancient methods validated → performs blessing ceremony at restoration completion
- **Gameplay:** Teaches traditional planting calendars, reveals sacred sites, explains cultural significance
- **Friendship Rewards:** Rare native species, ceremonial events, cultural history unlocks

**6. Captain Rosa — The Eco-Tourism Operator**
- **Role:** Economic ally, tourism development
- **Story Arc:** Struggling tour business → partners for eco-tours → expands successful sustainable tourism model
- **Gameplay:** Brings tourists to your farm, generates income, spreads reputation
- **Friendship Rewards:** Tourism income share, boat access, marketing boosts

**Friendship System:**
- **Relationship Levels:** Stranger → Acquaintance → Friend → Close Friend → Best Friend
- **Progression:** Gift-giving, quest completion, repeated interactions
- **Heart Events:** Story cutscenes at each friendship tier
- **Practical Benefits:** Discounts, gifts, unlocks, assistance

**Quest System:**
- **Main Story Quests:** Advance narrative, unlock zones
- **NPC Personal Quests:** Deepen relationships, learn stories
- **Scientific Quests:** "Collect 50 water samples," "Document 10 species"
- **Community Quests:** "Supply market with 100 oysters," "Host school field trip"
- **Seasonal Quests:** Time-limited challenges tied to events

**Town Reputation Meter:**
- **Scale:** 0-100 points
- **Factors:** Quest completion, market sales, town investment, ecosystem health visible from town
- **Thresholds:**
  - 0-20: Unknown stranger
  - 21-40: Curious experiment
  - 41-60: Valued contributor
  - 61-80: Town hero
  - 81-100: Regional legend
- **Benefits:** Discounts, volunteer help, policy support, regional fame

**Festivals & Events:**
- **Spring:** Planting Festival (celebrates new growth)
- **Summer:** Seafood Festival (showcases sustainable harvest)
- **Fall:** Migration Festival (celebrates species returning)
- **Winter:** Tide Festival (celebrates storm resilience)
- **Special Events:** Your farm's anniversary, species milestones, carbon achievements

### 6. Research & Tech Tree

**Research System Overview:**

Grandmother's journal contains research notes that unlock four skill branches. Players earn research points through observation, experimentation, and achieving milestones.

**Research Point Acquisition:**
- **Species Discovery:** 10 points per new species documented
- **Carbon Milestones:** 50 points per major carbon achievement
- **Ecosystem Health:** 25 points when zone reaches "Thriving" status
- **Scientific Observation:** 5 points per completed monitoring task
- **Experimentation:** 15 points for trying new planting methods
- **Story Progression:** Variable points for completing narrative beats

**Branch 1: Ecology**
*Understanding species interactions and ecosystem resilience*

**Tier 1 (Early Game):**
1. Basic Species Identification (Cost: 50 pts) — Unlock species catalog
2. Water Quality Testing (Cost: 75 pts) — Monitor salinity, pH, temperature
3. Sediment Analysis (Cost: 100 pts) — Understand substrate composition

**Tier 2 (Mid Game):**
4. Food Web Dynamics (Cost: 150 pts) — Visualize trophic interactions
5. Symbiosis Recognition (Cost: 175 pts) — Unlock symbiosis bonuses
6. Invasive Species Control (Cost: 200 pts) — Advanced removal techniques

**Tier 3 (Late Game):**
7. Biodiversity Optimization (Cost: 250 pts) — Max species packing strategies
8. Trophic Cascade Management (Cost: 275 pts) — Apex predator reintroduction
9. Ecosystem Resilience Theory (Cost: 300 pts) — Bonus to storm/disturbance recovery

**Tier 4 (Endgame):**
10. Assisted Evolution (Cost: 400 pts) — Breed climate-adapted varieties
11. Restoration Ecology Mastery (Cost: 500 pts) — All ecology bonuses +20%

**Branch 2: Engineering**
*Building structures and managing physical processes*

**Tier 1 (Early Game):**
1. Basic Sediment Management (Cost: 50 pts) — Prevent erosion
2. Tide Pool Creation (Cost: 75 pts) — Build artificial tide pools
3. Nursery Infrastructure (Cost: 100 pts) — Expand nursery capacity

**Tier 2 (Mid Game):**
4. Living Shorelines (Cost: 150 pts) — Natural coastal protection structures
5. Oyster Reef Breakwaters (Cost: 175 pts) — Wave attenuation reefs
6. Sediment Traps (Cost: 200 pts) — Accelerate land building

**Tier 3 (Late Game):**
7. Hydrological Restoration (Cost: 250 pts) — Reconnect tidal flows
8. Artificial Reef Engineering (Cost: 275 pts) — Substrate for kelp attachment
9. Climate Adaptation Infrastructure (Cost: 300 pts) — Heat/storm resilience

**Tier 4 (Endgame):**
10. Mega-Restoration Projects (Cost: 400 pts) — Large-scale transformations
11. Engineering Mastery (Cost: 500 pts) — All engineering bonuses +20%

**Branch 3: Policy**
*Navigating regulations, markets, and governance*

**Tier 1 (Early Game):**
1. Carbon Credit Basics (Cost: 50 pts) — Unlock credit certification
2. Sustainable Harvest Certification (Cost: 75 pts) — Premium market prices
3. Educational Outreach (Cost: 100 pts) — School groups visit farm

**Tier 2 (Mid Game):**
4. Carbon Market Analysis (Cost: 150 pts) — Price forecasting tool
5. Marine Protected Area Designation (Cost: 175 pts) — Legal protection for zones
6. Fishing Regulation Partnership (Cost: 200 pts) — Collaborative management

**Tier 3 (Late Game):**
7. Regional Policy Advocacy (Cost: 250 pts) — Influence local policy
8. Blue Carbon Policy Initiative (Cost: 275 pts) — National recognition
9. International Certification (Cost: 300 pts) — Global carbon market access

**Tier 4 (Endgame):**
10. Policy Innovation (Cost: 400 pts) — Create new conservation models
11. Policy Mastery (Cost: 500 pts) — All policy bonuses +20%

**Branch 4: Culture**
*Traditional knowledge, community, and education*

**Tier 1 (Early Game):**
1. Oral History Collection (Cost: 50 pts) — Learn from elder stories
2. Traditional Planting Calendar (Cost: 75 pts) — Seasonal wisdom bonuses
3. Community Workshops (Cost: 100 pts) — Volunteer labor pool

**Tier 2 (Mid Game):**
4. Indigenous Plant Varieties (Cost: 150 pts) — Access rare native species
5. Traditional Land Management (Cost: 175 pts) — Cultural burning, pruning techniques
6. Community Education Programs (Cost: 200 pts) — Reputation bonuses

**Tier 3 (Late Game):**
7. Cultural Heritage Sites (Cost: 250 pts) — Discover and protect sacred areas
8. Intergenerational Knowledge Transfer (Cost: 275 pts) — Youth apprentices
9. Regional Cultural Network (Cost: 300 pts) — Multi-community collaboration

**Tier 4 (Endgame):**
10. Cultural Restoration Model (Cost: 400 pts) — Holistic approach recognition
11. Culture Mastery (Cost: 500 pts) — All culture bonuses +20%

**Cross-Branch Prerequisites:**
Some advanced nodes require unlocks from multiple branches:
- **Climate-Adapted Restoration:** Ecology Tier 3 + Engineering Tier 2
- **Community-Based Management:** Policy Tier 2 + Culture Tier 2
- **Holistic Ecosystem Design:** All branches Tier 2

## Game Progression Arc

### Year 1: THE SHALLOWS — Foundation
**Season:** Spring (Start)

**Goals:**
- Learn basic planting mechanics
- Understand tidal cycles
- Plant first seagrass beds (1 hectare minimum)
- Meet all core NPCs
- Discover grandmother's vision through first journal entries
- Survive first small storm

**Key Moments:**
- **Opening:** Arrive at degraded property, meet Old Salt who explains the coast's decline
- **First Plant:** Tutorial planting sequence, satisfying growth animation
- **Journal Discovery:** Find grandmother's first entry, learn her dream
- **Storm Impact:** See how bare coastline suffers while your small seagrass patch holds sediment
- **First Harvest:** Collect first carbon credit payment

**Unlocks:**
- Nursery system
- Basic research tree (Tier 1)
- Market access
- NPC friendship system

---

### Year 2: THE MUDFLATS — Expansion
**Season:** Spring (Year 2)

**Goals:**
- Expand into salt marsh restoration (2 hectares)
- First carbon credit sale
- Town begins to acknowledge your work
- Unlock research lab partnership with Dr. Chen
- Complete at least 3 NPC quests

**Key Moments:**
- **Mudflats Unlock:** Mayor grants permit after seeing Year 1 success
- **Carbon Payment:** First substantial income from carbon credits
- **Scientific Partnership:** Dr. Chen proposes research collaboration
- **Market Success:** Your oysters gain "premium ecosystem-grown" reputation
- **Community Shift:** NPCs start believing in restoration

**Unlocks:**

## UI/UX Design

### Visual Design Philosophy
- **Nature-Inspired:** UI elements evoke driftwood, watercolor, hand-drawn maps
- **Minimal HUD:** During exploration, only essential info visible
- **Expandable Dashboard:** Deep information available when desired
- **Accessibility First:** Designed for wide range of players

### Core UI Elements

**Exploration HUD (Minimal):**
- **Top Left:** Time, date, weather icon
- **Top Right:** Stamina bar (subtle wave animation)
- **Bottom Center:** Tool radial menu (appears on input)
- **Bottom Right:** Quick inventory (seed count, current tool)

**Carbon Dashboard (Expandable):**
- **Main Graph:** Living line chart of carbon sequestration over time
- **Ecosystem Breakdown:** Pie chart by zone contribution
- **Current Rate:** "Tons CO₂/year" prominently displayed
- **Milestones:** Progress bars to next achievement
- **Comparison:** "Equal to X cars offset"
- **Market Price:** Current carbon credit value

**Ecosystem Health Panel:**
- **Biodiversity Score:** 0-100 with color gradient (red→yellow→green)
- **Species Count:** Icons for each species category
- **Water Quality:** Salinity, pH, temperature gauges
- **Sediment Quality:** Organic content, stability meters
- **Warnings:** Red alerts for issues (invasive species, poor water quality)

**Map System:**
- **Zone Overview:** All five zones visible at once
- **Health Color-Coding:** Zones colored by ecosystem health
- **Zoom Levels:** World view → Zone view → Plot view
- **Species Indicators:** Icons showing wildlife presence
- **Quest Markers:** Active objectives highlighted

**Radial Tool Menu:**
- **Center:** Currently equipped tool
- **8 Directions:** Quick-select up to 8 tools
- **Visual Icons:** Clear, color-coded tool icons
- **Controller/Keyboard/Mouse:** Works with all inputs
- **Context-Sensitive:** Available tools change by location

**Inventory System:**
- **Tabs:** Seeds, Tools, Harvested Goods, Equipment
- **Sorting:** By type, alphabetical, rarity
- **Stacking:** Similar items stack with count
- **Tooltips:** Hover for detailed info
- **Quick Transfer:** To storage, market, NPCs

**Dialogue System:**
- **Portrait + Text Box:** Character portrait with emotion variants
- **Name Display:** Character name with relationship heart level
- **Choice Options:** 2-4 choices for player responses
- **Journal Integration:** Important info auto-saved to journal
- **Skip Option:** Replay-friendly

**Research Tree Visualization:**
- **Four Branches:** Distinct visual styling per branch (nature, engineering, policy, culture)
- **Node States:** Locked (grayed), available (highlighted), unlocked (glowing)
- **Connections:** Lines showing prerequisites
- **Hover Info:** Detailed description and cost
- **Progress Tracker:** Research points displayed prominently

### Accessibility Features
- **Colorblind Modes:** Deuteranopia, Protanopia, Tritanopia support
- **Text Scaling:** 100%, 125%, 150%, 200%
- **Button Remapping:** Full controller and keyboard customization
- **Screen Reader Support:** UI narration for vision impairment
- **Subtitle Options:** Size, background, positioning
- **Motion Reduction:** Option to reduce particle effects, screen shake
- **Difficulty Options:** Adjust stamina drain, growth rates, income rates

### Tutorial Integration
- **No Separate Tutorial:** Mechanics introduced through Year 1 narrative
- **Grandmother's Journal:** Acts as tutorial guide
- **NPC Teaching:** Old Salt teaches fishing, Dr. Chen explains science
- **Progressive Unlocks:** Features introduced gradually, not overwhelming
- **Hint System:** Optional subtle hints if player seems stuck
- **Tutorial Replay:** Access tutorial info anytime in journal

## Audio Design

### Music Philosophy
"The soundtrack should feel like sitting by the ocean at dusk — peaceful, reflective, with moments of wonder."

### Musical Style
- **Core Genre:** Lo-fi ambient + acoustic instrumentation
- **Instruments:** 
  - Acoustic guitar (fingerpicking)
  - Piano (sparse, melodic)
  - Woodwinds (flute, clarinet)
  - Subtle strings
  - Field recordings (waves, birds, wind)
- **No Lyrics:** Purely instrumental to maintain peaceful ambiance
- **Dynamic Range:** Quiet moments + emotional swells

### Zone-Specific Themes

**The Shallows:**
- Light, bright acoustic guitar
- Gentle marimba accents
- Calm water sounds
- Morning/beginning feeling

**The Mudflats:**
- Earthy bass tones
- Rhythmic patterns mimicking tidal flow
- Reed instruments (oboe, clarinet)
- Afternoon sun feeling

**The Estuary:**
- Lush, layered instrumentation
- Mysterious filtered melodies
- Rich, warm harmonies
- Dappled light feeling

**The Reef Edge:**
- Flowing, continuous melodies
- Cello for depth
- Crystalline piano notes
- Cool, clear water feeling

**The Deep:**
- Ambient, spacious soundscapes
- Minimal percussion
- Reverb-heavy instrumentation
- Bioluminescent wonder feeling

### Dynamic Music System
- **Ecosystem Health:** Music richness increases with biodiversity
- **Weather Response:** Storm themes replace calm themes during weather events
- **Time of Day:** Different musical motifs for dawn, day, dusk, night
- **Seasonal Variation:** Each season has distinct instrumentation
- **Event Music:** Special themes for festivals, storms, major story beats

### Ambient Sound Design

**Water Sounds:**
- **Wave Types:** Gentle lapping, medium surf, storm waves
- **Tidal Movement:** Subtle flow sounds as tide changes
- **Rain:** Various intensities from drizzle to downpour
- **Underwater:** Muffled, ethereal ambiance when diving

**Wildlife Sounds:**
- **Birds:** Seagulls, terns, herons — realistic calls
- **Marine Mammals:** Distant whale songs, dolphin clicks
- **Fish:** Subtle splashing, jumping
- **Crabs:** Skittering on rocks
- **Insects:** Marsh sounds at dusk
- **Biodiversity Chorus:** More species = richer soundscape

**Environmental Sounds:**
- **Wind:** Through grass, in trees, across water
- **Vegetation:** Rustling mangroves, swaying seagrass (underwater)
- **Thunder:** Distant rolling to close crashes
- **Storm Surge:** Powerful wave impacts
- **Boat:** Engine sounds, oars, sailing

### UI Sound Design
- **Menu Navigation:** Soft water droplet sounds
- **Selection Confirm:** Gentle chime
- **Tool Equip:** Natural foley (picking up spade, etc.)
- **Planting:** Satisfying "plop" + sediment sound
- **Growth Milestone:** Magical shimmer
- **Carbon Counter:** Ascending tone as numbers increase
- **Achievement Unlock:** Uplifting musical phrase

### Audio Implementation
- **Adaptive Audio Middleware:** FMOD or Wwise for dynamic mixing
- **Spatial Audio:** 3D positioning for wildlife, environmental sounds
- **Layered Mixing:** Separate volume controls for Music, Ambiance, SFX, UI
- **Weather Transitions:** Smooth crossfades between weather states
- **Time-of-Day Mixing:** Gentle shifts as day progresses
